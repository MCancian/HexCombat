IJFS — Joint / Air-Missile Fires (D4)

Human-readable summary. Machine reference: docs/systems/ijfs.md. Port-fidelity audit, Area 6 of 10 — the largest phase.

What this system does

Models the PLA's pre-invasion and ongoing air & missile campaign against Taiwan. Sensors detect targets, doctrine picks what to hit and with what munition, fires are budgeted per platform, each strike rolls to destroy or suppress, and air defenses degrade over time. Capability ramps up over a multi-day "warmup," with an exquisite-intel boost on the opening day. Results feed forward: suppressed or destroyed coastal anti-ship launchers fire less in the naval crossing (D3).

The daily pipeline

  1. Build the day's run context (warmup day, z-day).
  2. Detection — sensors (satellite, aircraft) detect target IDs.
  3. Air-defense health — compute Taiwan's AD state.
  4. Targeting — choose targets & munitions by doctrine; apply exquisite intel on the warmup.
  5. Engagement — SEAD against air defenses (free shot, return fire).
  6. Strike — per-target probability of destroy/suppress, resolved by dice, within firing-capacity budgets.
  7. Writeback — aggregate per-(theater, type) results for downstream phases.
✅ Faithful port — verified. 10 of the 11 stages are 1:1 ports of TIV's ijfs_standalone modules (detection, targeting, engagement, strike probability & resolution, firing capacity, AD health, warmup profiles, loaders, daily state, run context). The orchestrator independently confirmed the pipeline order and the strike-probability model. The one adaptation: anti-ship targets are built from pre-loaded containers rather than TIV's SQLite default- targets path. Covered by the ported IJFS tests.

✅ IJFS now thins ground troops (D4-H linkage closed).

The ROC's ground battalions are themselves IJFS targets ("Maneuver Units," one per battalion instance, tagged with matching brigade/battalion IDs), so the air/missile campaign can find and destroy them. Each turn the "maneuver casualties" writeback is consumed — the struck battalions are removed from the order of battle before ground combat resolves, so strikes genuinely thin the brigades that fight. Detectability is biased by unit type (less-mobile/softer units are easier to find and kill) and by recent activity: a brigade that moved or fought last turn is more exposed. The anti-ship half of the writeback is likewise wired (it suppresses coastal launchers in the naval crossing).
