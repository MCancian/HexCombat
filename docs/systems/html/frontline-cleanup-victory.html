Front-line, Cleanup & Victory (D5)

Human-readable summary. Machine reference: docs/systems/frontline-cleanup-victory.md. Port-fidelity audit, Area 7 of 10.

What these systems do

Three end-of-turn systems. Front line: a drawn line is sampled into a hex sequence and the attacker's maneuver brigades are spread along it. Cleanup: each hex's owner (red / green / contested / none) is recomputed from who occupies it. Victory: at the very end of cleanup, a census of battalions on Taiwan decides the game — China loses if it has zero battalions ashore, and wins if it outnumbers the defenders.

✅ Front-line & cleanup are faithful ports. Polyline→hex sampling (2 km steps, nearest-hex), arc-length interpolation, and ownership recomputation match TIV's front_line_service.py / cleanup_hex_service.py (verified). Victory conditions are a HexCombat-original design (settled 2026-06-28) — TIV's ground phase has no victory check — and surface through game_over/winner on the turn result and the LLM observation.

Notes / deferrals:
  • Victory census counts all placed hexes (taiwan_hexes: null) — correct for the main-island scenario; distinguishing offshore islands needs terrain/land data (a later phase).
  • Front-line distribution is brigade-level and snaps to hex centers; TIV places at battalion level with finer per-hex clipping. A refinement for when the draw UI lands.
  • The front-line drawing UI is deferred to the graphics track; the logic exists for headless / LLM callers but there's no in-game drawing tool yet.
