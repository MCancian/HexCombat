Anti-ship & Mine Warfare (D3)

Human-readable summary. Machine reference: docs/systems/antiship-mine.md. Port-fidelity audit, Area 5 of 10.

What this system does

As the PLA invasion fleet crosses the strait, Taiwan's coastal anti-ship missiles fire at it, and sea mines threaten the approach to each beach. This system sinks/damages ships, and those losses turn into battalions lost at sea (fewer troops reach the beach). It runs each turn after the air/missile phase (which can suppress the defenders' launchers) and before the landing.

Two halves

  1. Missile crossing — a 6-stage pipeline: launch → in-flight failures → escort interception → homing/targeting → terminal defense → damage (fresh→damaged→sunk hulls). Decoys and escorts soak hits.
  2. Mine lane — mines are scattered in a field at each beach; the fleet takes a random approach path, and only mines near that path line are dangerous. Minesweepers clear a few; then the fleet runs the lane — decoys first (absorbing detonations like sponges), then real ships by increasing value — until the lane is open.
✅ Faithful ports from two source repos.
  • The missile crossing, firing plan, launch attrition, and magazine port TaiwanInvasionViewer's antiship_*.py stage-for-stage (verified) and are covered by the ported tests.
  • The geometric mine model ports the sister repo TaiwanDefenseRefactor/mine_warfare.py (verified those functions exist), adapted to HexCombat's per-turn fleet.
Deliberate design choices (documented, not bugs):
  • Mine source: HexCombat uses the sister repo's richer geometric mine model rather than TIV's own simpler sweep-based one — a conscious cross-repo choice (the "push a lane" premise the user asked for).
  • Count-based crossing: ships are tracked as per-type counts; per-hull escort magazine depletion exists as an overlay but is tuned not to bind. The deeper per-missile pipeline from the sister repo is intentionally deferred.
  • Per-category mine lethality: neutralization uses a high/medium/low table; a per-ship-type field would be more faithful (logged as a refinement).
  • Magazine gating isn't yet persisted across turns (passed empty for now).
